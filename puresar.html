Our Heritage

Hand-Braided
by Egyptian Hands.

The Nile Delta has grown banana trees for centuries. Each year, after harvest, millions of tonnes of banana stems are discarded — burned or left to decay. We saw not waste, but wealth.

Three generations of Egyptian artisans work under the Puresar name. Each piece takes between 40 and 120 hours to complete. Each knot is a signature pressed into fiber. Each product is numbered and certified.

🌾
The Raw Material

Banana stems harvested from Nile Delta farms. Zero chemical processing.

🤲
The Craft

Traditional Egyptian braiding refined for global luxury markets.

🏅
The Standard

Every piece numbered, signed, and given its Digital Twin certificate.

🌍
The Mission

Fair wages for Egyptian artisans. Regenerative farming. Zero waste.

From Earth to Object

Five Steps from Farm to Fiber.

🌿

Harvest

Banana stems collected from partner farms across the Nile Delta, post-fruit harvest

🔄

Extraction

Chemical-free water retting process separates long fiber strands naturally

☀️

Sun Drying

Dried on elevated racks under Egyptian sun. No synthetic heat, ever.

🧶

Crafting

Hand-spun and braided by certified Egyptian artisans. 40–120 hours per piece

✦

Certification

Numbered, signed, and issued its Digital Twin blockchain certificate

Environmental Impact

Luxury That Gives Back.

Every product you buy removes agricultural waste from the Nile Delta, supports an Egyptian artisan family, and replaces a synthetic alternative. This is not greenwashing — these are measurable outcomes.

The banana plant is nature's most efficient fiber producer. It grows in 9 months, requires no replanting, uses existing water systems, and its post-harvest waste is our primary material.
